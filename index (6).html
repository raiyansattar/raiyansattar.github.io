Raiyan Sattar

Undergraduate Student

Driven and ambitious third-year biology student at University of California, Riverside with a strong passion for emergency medicine, patient care, and clinical healthcare. Through a combination of academic achievement, clinical exposure, leadership involvement, and undergraduate research, I have developed a strong understanding of the role science and compassionate care play in medicine. My experiences as a certified EMT, hospital volunteer, researcher, and healthcare operations leader have strengthened my ability to work effectively in fast-paced environments while maintaining professionalism, empathy, and attention to detail. Currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Biology, I have built a strong academic foundation in physiology, cellular biology, genetics, and biochemistry while balancing rigorous coursework with clinical and extracurricular commitments. In addition to my studies, I conduct undergraduate research at both University of California, Riverside and University of California, Davis. These research experiences have allowed me to develop laboratory and analytical skills including DNA and RNA extraction, spectrophotometry, scientific documentation, and data interpretation. Collaborating alongside PhD researchers has strengthened my understanding of evidence-based medicine, scientific problem-solving, and teamwork while teaching me the importance of precision, organization, and reproducibility in research.

My passion for healthcare extends beyond academics and into direct patient care. As a certified EMT with BLS, CPR, and AED training, I have developed critical thinking skills, situational awareness, and the ability to remain calm under pressure. Working in healthcare settings has reinforced the importance of communication, teamwork, and compassion when caring for patients during vulnerable moments. Additionally, my volunteer work at Loma Linda University Health has provided valuable exposure to hospital operations, patient transport, clinical documentation, and interdisciplinary collaboration among healthcare teams. Outside of the hospital setting, I co-founded SWIFT Health, a non-emergency medical transportation company where I coordinated transportation and healthcare logistics for dozens of patients weekly. This experience strengthened my leadership, organizational, and communication skills while teaching me the importance of HIPAA compliance, patient confidentiality, and efficient healthcare coordination. I also gained firsthand experience managing operations in time-sensitive healthcare environments and communicating directly with clinics, hospitals, and care coordinators. Beyond healthcare and academics, I remain actively involved in leadership and campus engagement. As President of the Riverside Bhangra Team and Committee Chair for the Pakistani Student Association, I have organized cultural events, managed teams, coordinated logistics, and fostered inclusive environments that celebrate diversity and community. These experiences have strengthened my leadership abilities, adaptability, and commitment to serving others and my community. My long-term goal is to pursue a career in emergency medicine where I can combine scientific knowledge, clinical skill, leadership, and compassionate patient care to make a meaningful impact on diverse communities.

Through every academic, clinical, and leadership opportunity, I continue to challenge myself to grow professionally while preparing for a future in emergency medicine. I value resilience, discipline, and lifelong learning, and I strive to approach every experience with curiosity and purpose. Whether working with patients, collaborating with researchers, or leading student organizations, I aim to contribute positively to those around me while continuing to strengthen my dedication to healthcare and service.
